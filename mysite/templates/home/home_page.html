About Me

I wasn't exactly a model student in high school, but I used to like books. A lot, actually. At first, it was just a way to escape a disappointing reality. I liked philosophy. The kind that comes from life experiences and use a simple yet brilliant language to give actionable advice. The older the book was, the better. Philosophy problems don't change through time, but modernity has clouded our interpretation of them.

Those old philosophy books opened my eyes to a concerning trend. The business models that moved billions of lives online, based on using platforms for free in exchange for navigation data, were backfiring on a whole generation. It was 2017. Cambridge Analytica scandal came out. I was deeply disappointed and I needed to understand how those models worked. This is how my passion for math, technology and business began: a disappointment that later translated in the desire to make a change, because through my studies I realized how many exciting solutions to real-world problems can be found when integrating these fields.

Life has shuttered me around a few cities in Europe. I laid down interdisciplinary engineering foundations between Milan and The Netherlands, while exploring with particular intensity complex systems, data science and modelling. I worked on making sense of how the Lithium-Ion battery industry is developing in The Old Continent while the energy crisis was going on, giving me the chance to gain new perspectives on energy systems' dynamics. During my time in Amazon, I worked in Business Intelligence for the Transportation department, and had the chance to mess around with how packages are delivered to your door (sorry for the delays I might have caused).

I am currently located in Cambridge, MA, where I am pursuing a Master's in data science at MIT while also working as a research assistant. MIT offers me many opportunities to explore cutting-edge technology, and I am primarly focused on Deep Learning and Optimization.

This must not be considered my professional page, as I may digress into not serious stuff. It is just my little spot on the World Wide Web (version 2.0) where I share what I work on in the format I prefer. Also, I might or might not talk about cryptocurrencies related stuff, as they give me hope for a future in which the value created from internet interactions is better distributed.
